Question 1

True or False: Hyperloop was invented by Elon Musk, the founder of SpaceX.

True
False

The correct answer is false: Elon Must published a white paper outlining the technical and financial details of building a Hyperloop public transportation system between San Francisco and Los Angeles, but tubular pneumatic propulsion transportation systems were first proposed as early as 1799 and a few semi-vacuum locomotive systems were built and run in the mid 1840s.

Question 2

True or False: Supersonic ground transportation is only possible with electromagnetic levitation.

True
False

The correct answer is false: The key to supersonic speeds at sea level is reducing air friction, therefore the vacuum tube is the only required portion of Hyperloop transportation. In fact, Elon Musk's original proposal was based on air bearings. Certainly the most common propulsion and levitation sub-systems are a combination of linear induction motors and electromagnets. However, some student teams have even experimented with designing wheel-based pods. That, of course, won't be possible once the pods are able to reach speeds over 300 mph, but as long as they don't burn up, wheels are quite effective.

Fun fact: Linear induction motors are already used today in many amusement park rides across the United States.

Question 3

Between the 60+ members on the Carnegie Mellon Hyperloop team, how many different schools are represented?


The correct answer is 5: CMU Hyperloop is the only club on campus that has members from 5 different CMU colleges: College of Engineering, Integrated Innovation Institute, Tepper Business School, School of Design and School of Drama.


The site is in honor of the CMU Hyperloop team which competed at SpaceX headquarters on January 28th and placed 8 out of 27 other teams. Congratulations, team!
